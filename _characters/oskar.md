Oskar cannot play chess. He has tried, repeatedly, with great enthusiasm and consistently poor results. He keeps showing up anyway.

## Traits
- Comic relief -- reliably in over his head, never discouraged by it
- Chess ability ranges from bad to slightly less bad, depending on the day
- Travels with the main crew and throws himself into every situation
- Plays as a pawn in games -- usually the first one sacrificed, never seems to mind

## Background
Where Argus is steady, Oskar is chaotic. He joined the crew the way he joins most things -- by simply appearing and refusing to leave. Nobody remembers quite when he became a permanent fixture, but the journey would be quieter without him, and quieter is not always better.

## Role in the Story
Oskar is the crew's levity. His willingness to fail loudly and try again makes even the most tense moments breathable. In a story full of ambition and pressure, he is the reminder that showing up matters more than playing it perfectly.

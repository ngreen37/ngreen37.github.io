The world of *Princess and the Journey to Chess City* is shaped by the game people play -- and how far they are willing to go to play a better one.

## Cities

### [Checker Town](/locations/checker-town/)
Where the story begins. A struggling community built from fallen checker pieces, where hope is in short supply and most people never leave.

### [Chess City](/locations/chess-city/)
The destination. A prosperous city across the sea -- but entry requires winning chess tournaments, and visits require passing through security and getting Tagged.

### [Shogi Island](/locations/shogi-island/)
A nearby island where almost no one plays chess. Shogi rules here, and everyone speaks Japanese.

### [??? (Unknown City)](/locations/mystery-city/)
Spoken of only in rumors. Said to be the Capital of Chess -- and the source of the checker pieces that built Checker Town.

## Workplaces

### [Chess City Elementary](/locations/chess-city-elementary/)
A school inside Chess City where the narrator works as a janitor and eventually teaches chess -- getting Tagged at the security checkpoint every single shift.

### [The Sand Mines](/locations/sand-mines/)
Hard labor on the outskirts. Worked mostly by Checker Town citizens, run by corporate interests, and very difficult to leave.
